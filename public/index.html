How this works, honestly. Layer 1 is a deterministic keyword engine over a small illustrative rule set, now including a cross-border perimeter check between client jurisdiction and product domicile — instant, offline, fully reproducible. Layer 2 routes the draft to a jurisdiction-specialist AI agent, given the client's sophistication and the use case, to catch what keywords miss; it reasons over the rule summaries it's given, it does not know today's regulations from memory, and it is not a source of legal truth. Layer 3 is the only genuinely live regulatory data here — a real-time FCA register check. The historical tracker logs every review for audit purposes; curated past cases are meant to inform future prompts as human-reviewed examples, not as an automatically self-updating model. Nothing here is legal advice, and every red or amber flag should route to a human reviewer.
